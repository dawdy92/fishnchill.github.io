Freediving BC Gear

Your essential guide to freediving gear for British Columbia and the Pacific Northwest

Diving into the rugged waters of the Pacific Northwest (PNW) is a rewarding experience—kelp forests, rocky reefs, and marine life unlike anywhere else. But it’s also cold, challenging, and unforgiving if you're not properly equipped. Whether you're exploring the coves around Vancouver Island or diving off the Washington coast, the right gear can make or break your dive.

1. Wetsuit: Your First Line of Defense

Cold water demands serious insulation. A 7mm two-piece wetsuit (high-waisted pants and a hooded jacket) is standard in the PNW. Opt for open-cell neoprene—it sticks to your skin and retains heat better than lined suits.

2. Mask & Snorkel: Keep It Simple, Keep It Functional

  • Mask: Low-volume freediving mask to minimize equalizing effort.
  • Snorkel: Simple J-shaped snorkel without purge valves for reliability.

3. Fins: Power in the Water

  • Start with short snorkeling fins or upgrade to long freediving fins made of plastic, fiberglass, or carbon fiber.
  • Use closed-heel fins with snug foot pockets to avoid energy loss.

4. Weight System: Stay Neutral and Safe

Thick wetsuits make you floaty. Use a rubber weight belt with a quick-release buckle. Expect to use 12–20 lbs depending on body type and suit thickness.

5. Gloves: Grip, Warmth, and Protection

Choose 5mm neoprene gloves to keep your hands warm and protect against rocks or marine life.

6. Socks or Booties: Warm Feet, Better Fit

5mm neoprene socks or booties prevent blisters and keep your feet toasty year-round.

Freediving in BC isn’t just a hobby—it’s a cold-water adventure. With the right equipment, you’ll unlock a breathtaking underwater world filled with life and serenity. Whether you're spearfishing or just exploring, stay warm, stay safe, and stay salty.
